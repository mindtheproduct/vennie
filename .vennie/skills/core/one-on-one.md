# 1:1 Preparation

Get more out of your 1:1s. Walk in with a plan, walk out with clear next steps.

## Before the 1:1

### Gather Context

Pull from the past week:

**Wins:**
- Check `06-Evidence/` for recent wins worth sharing upward
- Check completed tasks and shipped work
- "Anything land this week that your manager should know about?"

**Blockers:**
- Active projects with stalled status
- Decisions waiting on manager input
- Resource constraints or team friction

**Decisions Made:**
- Review `03-Decisions/` for recent decisions
- Especially flag any that were risky or could use validation
- "Any calls you made this week where you'd value a second opinion?"

**Career Development:**
- Any evidence worth surfacing for visibility?
- Upcoming growth opportunities to discuss?
- Feedback to request?

**Questions:**
- "What do you need from your manager this week?"
- Check for strategic ambiguity — things where you're not sure of the direction
- Company/team context you're missing

### Generate the Agenda

Produce 5-7 bullet points, ranked by importance:

```markdown
# 1:1 Agenda — [Date]
**With:** [Manager name]

## Must Discuss
1. [Most important — blocker or decision]
2. [Second priority]

## Should Discuss
3. [Win worth sharing]
4. [Question about direction]

## If Time
5. [Career development topic]
6. [Lower priority item]
7. [FYI / awareness item]
```

### Coaching Notes

Add a brief note on framing:
- "Lead with [win] — it sets a positive tone before raising [blocker]"
- "For [decision], frame it as 'here's what I decided and why' not 'what should I do?'"
- "Your manager responds well to data — bring the numbers on [topic]"

## After the 1:1

Prompt the user to capture takeaways:

"How'd it go? Let me capture the key stuff:"

1. **Decisions made** — anything decided goes to `03-Decisions/`
2. **Action items** — tasks go to `03-Tasks/` with owner and deadline
3. **Feedback received** — save to `06-Evidence/Feedback/`
4. **Context learned** — update relevant project or person pages
5. **Career signals** — any promotion, development, or trajectory signals go to career notes

## Pattern Tracking

Over time, track 1:1 patterns:
- Topics that keep coming up (might indicate unresolved issues)
- Action items that carry over week to week (accountability gap)
- Manager's recurring concerns (signals about what they value)

Surface these: "This is the third week [topic] has been on your agenda. Is it time to escalate or reframe it?"

## End With

Before the 1:1: "You're prepped. Lead with [top item]. If you only get 10 minutes, make sure you cover [must-discuss items]."

After capture: "Got it. [N] action items captured, [N] decisions logged. Anything else from the conversation?"
