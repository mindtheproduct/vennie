# Performance Review Preparation

Turn your evidence trail into a compelling self-assessment. No more "I can't remember what I did this quarter."

## How to Start

"When's your review? And what period does it cover?"

Then ask:
- "What format does your company use? (Free-form, structured rubric, specific questions?)"
- "Who's reviewing you? (Just manager, or 360?)"
- "What's your goal for this review? (Promotion case, raise justification, just a solid review, course correction?)"

## Data Gathering

Pull from everything available:

**Evidence folder (`06-Evidence/`):**
- Wins with metrics — these are your headliners
- Learnings — shows growth mindset
- Feedback received — external validation

**Decision log (`03-Decisions/`):**
- Decisions made and their outcomes
- Shows judgment, ownership, strategic thinking

**Projects (`04-Projects/`):**
- What shipped, what's in progress
- Cross-team collaboration evidence
- Scope and complexity of work

**People context (`05-People/`):**
- Who you influenced, mentored, collaborated with
- Stakeholder feedback signals

## Building the Self-Assessment

Organize evidence into themes. Common ones:

1. **Impact / Shipping** — what you delivered and the outcomes
2. **Technical/Product Excellence** — quality of your work, depth of craft
3. **Leadership & Influence** — how you moved people and decisions
4. **Growth & Learning** — how you leveled up, new skills
5. **Collaboration** — how you worked across teams

For each theme:
- Lead with the strongest evidence (metrics > anecdotes)
- Connect work to business outcomes ("Shipped X → Y% improvement in Z")
- Show trajectory, not just snapshots

## Gap Analysis

This is where Vennie earns its keep. Identify:

- "You have strong shipping evidence but limited stakeholder influence examples. Want to think about how to frame influence differently?"
- "Your evidence is weighted toward individual contribution. If promotion requires team leadership evidence, we should find examples."
- "I see 3 wins with metrics and 5 without. Can we add numbers to any of the others?"

Be honest about gaps. Better to know now and address them than get surprised in the review.

## Output Formats

Ask the user what they need:

- **Markdown** — clean, structured document
- **Google Doc-ready** — formatted for paste into Google Docs
- **Notion-ready** — with toggles and callouts
- **PDF-ready** — clean layout for printing/sharing
- **Bullet points** — for pasting into a review form

## Output Structure

```markdown
# Self-Assessment: [Review Period]
**Name:** [user]
**Period:** [start] — [end]
**Prepared:** [today]

## Summary
[2-3 sentence overview of the period — lead with impact]

## Key Accomplishments
### [Theme 1: e.g., Product Impact]
- [Accomplishment with metric]
- [Accomplishment with context]

### [Theme 2: e.g., Technical Leadership]
- ...

## Growth & Development
[Skills developed, challenges overcome, feedback incorporated]

## Looking Ahead
[Goals for next period, areas of focus, what you want to take on]
```

## Saving

- Save to `06-Evidence/Reviews/YYYY-[period]-self-assessment.md`

## End With

"Your strongest evidence is [X]. If I were your manager reading this, the thing that would stand out most is [Y]. The one area I'd prepare to discuss further is [Z] — have a story ready for that."
